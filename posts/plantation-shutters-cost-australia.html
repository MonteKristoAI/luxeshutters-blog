What Pushes the Price Up or Brings It Down?

Every dwelling is different. Over years of quoting across the Riverina, we've encountered everything from straightforward rectangles to elaborate heritage bays that consumed half a day just to template. So what actually nudges your louvre investment higher or lower? Essentially, these variables explain most of the variance in plantation shutters cost.

Factors That Increase the Outlay

  • Non-standard shapes: Arched, circular, or raked openings demand bespoke frames and cardboard templates. Consequently, expect 20-40% above the equivalent rectangular area.
  • Bay configurations: Three or more angled panels wrapping around a bay add significant complexity. Specifically, Chris templates the precise angles on site because even two degrees of misalignment ruins the fit.
  • Sliding or bi-fold track systems: Rail-mounted assemblies for glass sliders carry higher hardware expenses than standard hinged panels. Therefore, budget accordingly for larger apertures.
  • Stained timber finishes: If you'd prefer the natural grain visible rather than painted white, basswood stained typically adds $80-$120 per m2 on top.
  • Elevated or restricted-access openings: If scaffolding or specialised equipment is required, fitting naturally takes longer and consequently raises labour charges.

Factors That Reduce Your Spend

  • Whole-dwelling orders: Completing 8-12 openings in a single visit is far more efficient than scheduling three separate trips. As a result, we typically discount 10-15% off the per-opening rate for bulk jobs.
  • Polymer over timber: Honestly? For white-painted louvres, most homeowners can't distinguish PVC from basswood at arm's length. Choosing polymer therefore saves 30-40% with zero visible compromise.
  • Standard rectangular openings: Straightforward geometry means faster fitting and lower labour. It really is that simple.
  • Recess-fit mounting: Seating panels inside the reveal is quicker than face-mounting with an external architrave surround. So if your openings allow it, recess-fit trims both time and expense.

Which Material Should You Choose for Each Room?

Laboratory testing demonstrates that louvred panels slash heat flux through glazing by 20-58%, depending on the cavity depth and substrate composition (Luxaflex, 2026). That's a tangible difference on your quarterly electricity statement. However, which substrate belongs in which space?

PVC (Forte Select, from $300/m2): Easily our highest-volume product. If you're outfitting an entire residence on a sensible budget, this is where most Riverina families gravitate. Specifically, it excels in bathrooms, kitchens, laundries, and children's bedrooms where resilience trumps character grain.

ThermoPoly (Bayview ThermoPoly, from $350/m2): This composite is specifically formulated for harsh Australian conditions, sitting a notch above standard polymer in both thermal resistance and panel rigidity. Because Riverina afternoons frequently surpass 40 degrees while winter dawns bring genuine frost, that performance differential is tangible rather than theoretical. Basswood (Bayview Basswood, from $500/m2): Genuine hardwood carrying a tactile warmth and heft that engineered alternatives can't entirely replicate. First, imported panels frequently arrive with dimensional tolerances wider than domestic specifications permit. By contrast, CWGlobal products are fabricated domestically under Australian Standard AS2663.2, verified through the WSAA certification programme, and backed by enforceable local guarantees.
