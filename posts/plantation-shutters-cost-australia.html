What Makes the Price Go Up (or Down)?

We quote hundreds of jobs a year, and no two are exactly the same. Here's what actually moves the needle on your final price.

Factors That Increase Cost

  • Non-standard window shapes: Arched, circular, or raked windows need custom frames. Expect 20-40% more than a rectangular window of the same area.
  • Bay windows: Three or more panels that wrap around a bay add complexity. We need to measure precise angles on site.
  • Sliding or bi-fold configurations: Track-mounted shutters for sliding doors cost more than standard hinged panels.
  • Stained finishes: If you want the natural timber grain visible (not painted white), basswood stained finishes add $80-$120 per m2.
  • High or hard-to-reach windows: Second-storey windows that need scaffolding add to installation time.
  • Remote locations: We cover the entire Riverina, but jobs further from Temora may include a small travel component.

Factors That Can Save You Money

  • Whole-home orders: Fitting 8-12 windows in one visit is more efficient. We pass that saving on.
  • Standard rectangular windows: Simple shapes, simple price.
  • PVC over timber: Honestly, most people can't tell the difference once they're painted white. PVC saves 30-40% over basswood with no visible tradeoff on a white shutter.
  • Recess-fit installation: Fitting inside the window recess is faster than face-mounting outside the frame.

PVC vs Basswood vs ThermoPoly: Which Should You Choose?

After installing hundreds of sets across the Riverina, here's how we'd break it down. It's not about "best" overall. It's about what's right for each room.

PVC (Forte Select, from $300/m2): Our most popular option by a wide margin. Won't warp, handles moisture, easy to clean, and you genuinely can't tell it apart from painted timber at normal viewing distance. If you're fitting out a whole home on a budget, PVC is where most Riverina families land.

ThermoPoly (Bayview ThermoPoly, from $350/m2): Engineered for Australian conditions. It's a step up from standard PVC in terms of insulation and structural rigidity. Ideal for bathrooms, laundries, or if your home is in a high-humidity area. We fit a lot of these in the Riverina because summers are stinking hot and winters bring proper frost, so the thermal performance matters.

Basswood (Bayview Basswood, from $500/m2): Real timber. Lighter weight than PVC, with a warmer feel and natural grain (visible in stained finishes). The go-to for living rooms and bedrooms where you want that premium look. In our experience, basswood looks best in heritage homes and renovated weatherboards across Temora and Wagga.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are plantation shutters worth the money in Australia?

For most Australian homes, yes. While the upfront cost is higher than blinds or curtains, plantation shutters last 15-30 years without replacement, add directly to your property value as permanent fixtures, and reduce energy bills through better insulation. In regional areas like the Riverina, where you're heating in winter and cooling in summer, the energy savings add up. Industry estimates put the ROI at up to 75% over the life of the product.

How much do plantation shutters cost for a whole house?

A three-bedroom home with 8-10 standard windows typically costs $4,000-$12,000 for plantation shutters in Australia, depending on material choice. PVC across an average Riverina home runs $4,500-$7,500 installed. Basswood or stained timber pushes toward $8,000-$12,000. Whole-home orders usually get better per-window pricing because the installation is more efficient in one visit.

Which is cheaper, PVC or timber plantation shutters?

PVC is 30-40% cheaper than timber. Installed, PVC runs $300-$500 per square metre while basswood timber costs $500-$700 per m2. For most homes choosing white-painted shutters, there's no visible difference between PVC and timber at normal viewing distance. PVC also handles moisture and extreme heat better, which matters in Australian bathrooms and west-facing windows.

Do plantation shutters block heat in summer?

Plantation shutters significantly reduce heat gain through windows. When closed, the air gap between the shutter and the glass acts as insulation, similar in principle to double glazing. ThermoPoly shutters from CWGlobal are specifically engineered for thermal performance. In Riverina summers where temperatures regularly exceed 40 degrees, we've had customers report noticeable cooling improvements, especially on west-facing windows that cop full afternoon sun.

How long does it take to install plantation shutters?

From order to installation, expect 4-6 weeks for Australian-manufactured shutters (CWGlobal products). The actual fitting takes 1-3 hours for a standard room of 3-4 windows, or a full day for a whole-home installation of 10+ windows. We handle the complete process from measure to install, so you deal with one team from start to finish. Imported shutters from overseas can take 8-12 weeks.
