What Pushes the Price Up or Brings It Down?

No two homes are the same. After quoting hundreds of jobs across the Riverina, we've seen everything from simple rectangles to heritage bay windows that took half a day to measure. Here's what actually shifts the final number.

Factors That Increase Cost

  • Non-standard window shapes: Arched, circular, or raked windows need custom frames and templates. Expect 20-40% more than the same area in a rectangle.
  • Bay windows: Three or more angled panels wrapping around a bay add complexity. Chris measures the precise angles on site because even a couple of degrees matters.
  • Sliding or bi-fold configurations: Track-mounted shutters for sliding doors cost more than standard hinged panels due to the hardware.
  • Stained timber finishes: If you want the natural grain visible instead of painted white, basswood stained adds $80-$120 per m2.
  • Second-storey or hard-to-reach windows: If scaffolding or specialised access is needed, installation takes longer.

Factors That Save You Money

  • Whole-home orders: Fitting 8-12 windows in one visit is far more efficient than three separate trips. We pass that saving on, typically 10-15% off the per-window rate.
  • PVC over timber: Honestly? For white-painted shutters, most people can't tell PVC from basswood at normal viewing distance. PVC saves 30-40% with no visible tradeoff.
  • Standard rectangular windows: Simple shapes, faster install, lower cost. It's that straightforward.
  • Recess-fit installation: Fitting inside the window recess is quicker than face-mounting with an external frame.

Do Plantation Shutters Actually Save Energy in Australia?

The Australian Government's energy.gov.au reports that up to 40% of heating energy is lost through windows, and almost 90% of a home's heat gain enters through them (energy.gov.au, 2026). Plantation shutters create a trapped air layer between the louvre and the glass, acting as insulation. It's a similar principle to double glazing, but at a fraction of the cost.

The WincovER rating system, developed for Australian conditions, rates window coverings on a 10-star scale for both heating and cooling performance. Shutters consistently score well because of the adjustable louvres. Open them to let winter sun in. Close them to block summer heat. Tilt them for privacy without sacrificing airflow.

In the Riverina, where electricity prices keep climbing and the temperature range demands both heating and cooling, that flexibility is worth real money. We've had customers in Griffith tell us their air conditioner runs noticeably less after fitting ThermoPoly shutters across the west side of the house. Can we guarantee a specific dollar saving? No. But the physics is clear, and the government data backs it up.
